Saint George's Sword Care Guide

Soil for Saint George's Sword

Well-draining, sandy soil with organic matter.

Saint George's Sword prefers well-draining soil with a sandy texture, enriched with organic matter.

Watering

Regular watering, keeping the soil consistently moist.

Maintain consistent moisture to support the growth of Saint George's Sword, especially during dry periods.

Lighting

Bright, indirect light to partial shade.

Place the plant in a location with bright, indirect light or partial shade to promote healthy growth.

Benefits

Sword-shaped foliage with attractive patterns.

Saint George's Sword is known for its distinctive sword-shaped leaves with unique patterns.

Low maintenance and drought-tolerant.

This plant requires minimal care and can withstand periods of drought.

Non-Benefits

May be sensitive to overwatering; avoid waterlogged soil.
